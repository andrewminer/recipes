Lasagna

prep 30 minutes, cook 2½ hours
× 12
While sticking very close to the traditional formula, this recipe adds interest by adding some sausage and extra spices to make it really stand out.

1

  • 1 lb
    sausage, Italian
  • ¾ lb
    ground beef, lean
  • ½ cup
    onion (minced)
  • 2 tbsp
    garlic (minced)
In a dutch oven, cook the meat, onions, and garlic over a medium heat until browned.

2

  • 28 oz
    tomatoes, crushed
  • 12 oz
    tomato paste
  • 13 oz
    tomato purée
  • ½ cup
    water
  • 2 tbsp
    sugar, granulated
  • 1½ tsp
    basil, dried
  • ½ tsp
    fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp
    Italian seasoning
  • 1 tbsp
    salt
  • ¼ tsp
    pepper, black
  • 2 tbsp
    parsley, fresh (chopped)
Stir in the tomatoes, water, sugar, and spices. Cover and simmer for about 90 minutes, stirring occasionally.

3

  • 12
    lasagna noodles
Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook the noodles until they are slightly al dente (about 8–10 minutes). Drain the noodles and rinse in cold water.

4

Pre-heat the oven to 375°F.

5

  • 16 oz
    ricotta cheese
  • 1
    egg
  • ½ tsp
    salt
  • 2 tbsp
    parsley, fresh (chopped)
In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese with the egg, parsley, and salt.

6

  • ¾ lb
    mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • ¾ cup
    parmesan cheese (grated)
Spread 1½ cups of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9”⨉13” baking dish. Arrange half the noodles lengthwise over the sauce. Spread half the ricotta cheese mixure over the noodles. Cover with ⅓ of the mozzerella cheese. Spoon over another 1½ cups of meat sauce, and then layer with ¼ cup of parmesean cheese. Repeat the layers starting with the noodles.

7

Lightly grease a sheet of foil and cover the baking dish with the greased side down. Cook in the oven for 25 minutes, remove the foil, and then cook for another 25 minutes. Allow to cool for 15 minutes before serving.
